title: Gesture Mimicking Robot Arm
description:  The Gesture-Mimicking Robot Arm is a low-cost, sensor-driven robotic arm developed as a proof-of-concept for remote operation in dangerous or physically inaccessible environments, such as bomb disposal or surgical assistance. The system consists of two subsystems: a wearable sleeve-and-glove equipped with flex sensors and IMU accelerometers to capture finger and arm movement, and a robotic arm driven by precision motors that mirrors the user's movements in real time. Sensor data is wirelessly transmitted between two Raspberry Pis over Wi-Fi, allowing low-latency control with no physical tether. The arm features 3 degrees of freedom at the elbow and wrist joints, with a three-finger pulley-driven hand designed to replicate tendon-like motion, all built within a $300 budget.
